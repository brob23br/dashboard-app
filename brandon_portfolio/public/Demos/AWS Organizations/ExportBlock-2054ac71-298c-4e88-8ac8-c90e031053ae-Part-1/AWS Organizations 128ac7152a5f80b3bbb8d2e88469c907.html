AWS Organizations is a comprehensive account management service provided by Amazon Web Services (AWS) that enables businesses to centrally manage and govern their AWS environment across multiple accounts.

Key features and uses of AWS Organizations include:

  • Centralized management of multiple AWS accounts
  • Consolidated billing for all member accounts
  • Hierarchical grouping of accounts
  • Centralized policy-based controls
  • Automated account creation and management

AWS Organizations helps enterprises to scale their AWS operations efficiently, improve security, and optimize costs across their entire AWS infrastructure.

  1. From Console Home, navigate to AWS Organizations. Click Create an organization
  1. The account that I am currently logged in as has been allocated as the management account of the organization.

The management account of an AWS Organization has several important capabilities:

  • Create and manage the organizational structure, including creating new accounts and organizational units (OUs)
  • Apply service control policies (SCPs) to restrict permissions for member accounts
  • Enable and configure features like consolidated billing
  • Invite existing AWS accounts to join the organization
  • Remove member accounts from the organization
  • Access and manage all resources in member accounts (if granted appropriate permissions)
  • Set up and manage organization-wide services like AWS Control Tower or AWS Config

It's important to note that the management account has significant control over the entire organization, so access to it should be carefully restricted and monitored.
