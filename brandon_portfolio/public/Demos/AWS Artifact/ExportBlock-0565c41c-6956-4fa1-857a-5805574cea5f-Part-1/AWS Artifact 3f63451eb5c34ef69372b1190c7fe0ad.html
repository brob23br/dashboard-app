Overview

AWS Artifact is a self-service portal provided by Amazon Web Services (AWS) for on-demand access to AWS' compliance reports and agreements. It serves as a central resource for compliance-related information that matters to AWS customers.

Key Features of AWS Artifact

  • Compliance Reports: Provides access to AWS' security and compliance documents, such as ISO certifications, SOC reports, and PCI reports.
  • Agreements: Offers the ability to review, accept, and track the status of AWS agreements like the Business Associate Addendum (BAA).
  • On-Demand Access: Users can download compliance documents and accept agreements at any time, free of charge.
  • User-Friendly Interface: Easy-to-navigate portal for quick access to necessary compliance information.

Benefits

  • Streamlined Compliance: Simplifies the process of accessing and managing compliance documentation for AWS services.
  • Time and Cost Savings: Eliminates the need for lengthy processes to request and obtain compliance reports.
  • Up-to-Date Information: Provides the most current compliance information and reports available from AWS.
  • Support for Audits: Helps organizations prepare for audits by providing necessary compliance documentation.

AWS Artifact is an essential tool for businesses that need to demonstrate compliance with various regulations and standards while using AWS services.

Demo

  1. On the AWS Management Console, select Services. I already have it favorited, but if you don’t, scroll down on the left pane to Security, Identity, & Compliance. Then select AWS Artifact.
  1. On the AWS Artifact page, click View Reports.

3a. At the time of this exercise, there are 279 reports available. For this demo, we’re going to search for Systems and Organization Controls reports.

3b. SOC reports, also known as Service Organization Control (SOC) reports, are independent third-party examination reports that provide information about AWS's controls relevant to security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. These reports are essential for AWS customers to understand AWS's control environment and to support their compliance requirements.

SOC 1 reports specifically focus on controls at a service organization that are relevant to user entities' internal control over financial reporting. They help AWS customers and their auditors understand the controls AWS has in place that may affect their financial statements.

Downloading the report is not necessary for the demonstration.

  1. Next, we’ll look at Agreements. Agreements in AWS Artifact are legal documents that customers can review, accept, and manage directly through the AWS Artifact portal. These include:
  • Business Associate Addendum (BAA): For customers who need to comply with HIPAA regulations.
  • Nondisclosure Agreement (NDA): For accessing confidential AWS information.
  • Other compliance-related agreements: Such as those related to GDPR or specific industry standards.

These agreements allow customers to efficiently manage their compliance requirements with AWS services without the need for lengthy negotiation processes.
